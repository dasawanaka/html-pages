Type: (T/text-long)
As I indicated earlier the text has quite a few gaps that are clearly marked to be filled in - I mean here the whole sections are missing. In a sense the text should not contain the conclusions section since the research here looks unfinished. This is the major issue I have with the thesis. In addition, the central parts of the thesis, i.e., the elaboration of the features ontology, lack both the details and the required scope and depth. The author does not tell us whether the snip shown in Figure 3 covers all classes in the ontology. Are they any definitions like the ones shown in Figure 6? Do the OWL object properties mentioned in the thesis have any logical properties, i.e., whether they are functions or are asymmetrical? In my opinion this ontology should define classes related to geometry like cylinder or sphere. Some descriptions of engineering functions may be useful as well. Given that DOLCE is mentioned earlier I would expect embedding this ontology of features in some OWL version of DOLCE. The author should give access to the final version of the ontology (e.g., by uploading into a public repository like github) when it is finished otherwise the thesis may be seen as an exercise in futility. Finally, I would write the ontological formal definitions directly into the text, using, for example, the Manchester syntax, alongside screenshots from Protege. Also, I was not able to grasp the benefits of the ontological approach sketched in section 5.5. In particular I could not find the explicit link between the three types of holes defined in section 5.4 and the design issues described in 5.5. The author should be more verbose than just saying that ‘The system described in this experimental case study enhanced with the results of ontological analysis is able to overcome this problem. It allows the user to position wanted features first by the means of their ’seed’ geometry, i.e. by stating the location of the primary property of the feature.’ What kind of enhancement are we talking about here? Did you somehow import your OWL ontology into Grashopper? Etc. The thesis does not discuss whether there are any other ontologies of features or related notions, so the survey of state-of-the-art research is not satisfactory in this part. The text mentions IOF, but it does say anything on whether IOF provide any ontologies similar to the one presented in the thesis. The author should take care that every diagram that conveys important information is readable. This requirement is not satisfied by Figure 12, which is crucial to understand section 5.5.1. In particular I was not able to see the actual geometry of components 2 and 47. There are other, obvious, issues with formatting.
